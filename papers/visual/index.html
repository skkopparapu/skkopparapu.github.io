Formant Tracking in Speech using Visual Cues of a Spectrogram

Hrishikesh Karmarkar1, Sunil Kopparapu2, P. V. S. Rao3

Abstract:

Formant tracking in speech signal is an essential component of any speech recognition system. Formant tracking has been the subject of investigation by many researcher over the past many years. Several methods have been proposed in literature to track formants in a speech signal. Almost all the methods use either the time-energy or the frequency-energy information of the speech signal exclusively to track speech formants. Using only this information to track formants does not, in our opinion, amount to using all the information that is available from a speech signal. For this reason errors in estimation of formants crop in, for example, it is common for most formant tracking algorithms to miss formants in the Fourier spectrum in the absence of a suitable look-ahead in time. In this paper, we present a novel method to track formants using the the visual cues present in a speech spectrogram. We compare our formant tracking results with the commonly used formant tracking utility of the ESPS package and show that it is advantageous to track formants based on visual cues in our experiments.
