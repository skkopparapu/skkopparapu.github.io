Minimizing posteriori energy function

In general will be non-convex, mainly due to the non-convexity of the potential function (see for example (2.18)). Consequently, algorithms based on ideas of steepest descent would be prone to being trapped in a local minima. Also the presence of a binary line field precludes the possibility of using steepest descent type algorithms. At present a good option seems to be some kind of random search method, though such algorithms are extremely slow. One such algorithm which has gained a lot of popularity and has proved very effective in many problems, is the simulated annealing (SA) algorithm of Kirkpatrick et al [80] (see also Aarts and Korst [82]). We would like to mention that another algorithm due to Solis and Wets [92] referred to as the random optimization (RO) algorithm could also be used. From preliminary experiments conducted by us, it seems that the SA algorithm is faster than the RO algorithm, though one has to take care of some extra nuances of the SA algorithm.
