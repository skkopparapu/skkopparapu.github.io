EXPERIENCE

29.07.91 onwards
Pursuing research in the area of Image Processing and Computer Vision. Have developed a framework of modular integration and multiresolution to solve low-level and high-level vision problems. Emphasis of the research has been on integrating various modules that are involved in trying to solve a problem in computer vision. Also we have been looking into various problems like stereo vision, optical flow estimation, color image restoration in a Multiresolution (Image Pyramid / Wavelet) framework. We have used this developed framework to successfully extract depth information from stereo pair of images and also we have been successful in estimating optical flow from temorarly spaced images. The developed framework has been successfully tested for scene interpretation and color image restoration also. Prior to this we worked on an edge preserving adaptive algorithm for image restoration, the emphasis here was more on the speed of restoration in terms of time taken to restore while preserving discontinuities. In this process I have been required to conduct simulations for which I have been working on the Linux (public domain operating system; I was instrumental in installing this public domain software on the various 386 and 486 platforms available in the laboratory too.) platform and have extensively used the C language. I have been on the departmental computer networking committee and am involved in the maintenance of the machines in the lab in which I work in the capacity of a systems administrator.

Right now we are working on the joint problem of image segmentation and image interpretation using the HMM and as a team effort we are working on the problem of face identification.

04.07.89 - 25.07.91

Studied Artificial Neural Networks and successfully used this to classify sonar targets. Was in this capacity involved in developing software concerning classification using Neural Networks which was incorporated in the final module. The results were very encouraging in the sense the performance was far better than the knowledge based target classification which was done as a part of the project earlier. This work resulted in a publication.
